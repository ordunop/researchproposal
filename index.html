People started seeing the high school diploma become less effective at aiding in the employment process, and so people started going for bachelor's degrees. Introduction

The issue I will be examining is job security among first-generation college graduates. Whether or not a college education actually aids in helping first-generation students achieve a successful career. This is important to the education system and expectations that we have of young adults, college is pushed on so many people and yet we see so many young educated people struggling to get into their career of study. A focus point of the study will be to compare employment rates among white first-generation college graduates to the employment rates among Latinx first-generation college graduates. Latinx people have been known in the U.S as a driving force in hard labor jobs, but once they are put in competition with their white peers they seem to fall short (in terms of employment rates) in professional corporate jobs.
